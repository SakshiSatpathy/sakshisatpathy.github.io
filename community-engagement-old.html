Student Delegate at 62nd session of the United Nations Commission on the Status of the Women:
[March 2018]  https://www.unwomen.org/en/csw/csw62-2018
  • ​Student Speaker at the UN and co-writer of the Girls’ Statement to Delegations
  • UN CSW watchdog cataloging and analyzing international delegations’ references to “girls” and “women” when discussing specific national initiatives for female empowerment
  • GLI luncheon student ambassador who moderated and conversed with international ambassadors to Cuba, Bhutan, El Salvador, Qatar, Jordan, Pakistan about various approaches to gender parity, cultural differences and economic empowerment over lunch
  • Active member of a GLI advocacy group on recognizing signs of Girl Trafficking, an issue presented to activists and ambassadors our group met.
  • Attended, participated and spoke with ambassadors at workshops arranged by UN bodies: WHO, UNFCCC, UNICEF, UN Women, l’OIF. In particular, spoke with ambassadors to Ireland (the head of UN CSW), Thailand, Cameroun, learning more about the connection between health and ICTs, NCDs, how climate change disproportionately affects women, land rights, child sex exploitation and online extortion, child marriage and FGM, and gender parity advances and challenges in French speaking communities, among others. 
  • Attended, participated and spoke with activists at workshops arranged by NGOs: Amnesty International, the Feminist Majority Foundation, CARE, GAGE, UnchainedAtLast, The International Union for Land Value Taxation, NowThis, the AHA Foundation and others, learning more about diverse topics from incarceration inequality, indigenous rights, child marriage in the US and globally, honor killings, the education gap, among others.

​‘Believe In Me’ activist and Student speaker at California State Board of Education, Sacramento, July 13, 2016: 
I spoke and joined a peaceful demonstration as part of the racial justice activist organization ‘Californians for Justice’s  Believe In Me Campaign. We strived to add laws to the California curriculum that focused on both students’ social emotional learning and their academics, while providing additional funding to children who were in need of it (foster kids, low income kids, English learners). My speech urged SBE to mandate the California Student Climate Survey, assessing schools by how safe their environment is for students, including questions asking if students felt protected by their peers and if they had a trusted adult which would be taken anonymously—and would ensure that California schools make their school environments less hostile places in order for students to succeed at academics and graduation.
