  • Girl Scout Gold Award, Oct 2017:   www.project-greet.com 
    • I designed, implemented and shared my Gold Award Project, called Project GREET (Girl Rights: Engage, Empower, Train) which  countered girls’ rights issues--child marriage, human trafficking, gender inequity--locally, nationally and globally. Specifically, I created and shared films, a website, and YouTube playlist that educated on the root causes, prevalence, myths and how to counter child marriage, trafficking and gender inequity. Additionally, I created and distributed a curriculum 'Guidelines to Rehabilitate Young Trafficked Girls’ that provides activist organizations the steps to set up vocational training programs/fairs to economically empower girls at risk of/previously trafficked.
    • Through this project, I interviewed and communicated with various lawyers, social workers, teachers, and activists of varying ages and nationalities, and my films were screened at 59+ locations in 15 nations. My project artifacts have been shared with numerous local, national and international partner organizations, and with various global leaders, activists and interested parties at the 62nd session of UN CSW (United Nations Commission on the Status of Women). Project GREET impacted (directly and indirectly) an estimate of 3 million individuals.
