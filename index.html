Hello, my name is Sakshi Satpathy.

I am graduating from Columbia University in May 2025, where I am currently studying as a Master's in Computer Science student with a specialization in Vision, Graphics, Interaction, and Robotics (VGIR). ​I completed my undergraduate degree from the University of California, Berkeley, in May 2023. At UC Berkeley, I earned a triple-major in  Computer Science, Data Science and Economics. Additionally, I completed a minor in Education and earned the Berkeley Certificate of Design Innovation. I am currently interested in Machine Learning Engineer roles, particularly Computer Vision Engineer roles.

This interest in Computer Vision started early in UC Berkeley, where I took most Machine Learning courses offered, after which I took more specialized VGIR courses at Columbia. I have undertaken various projects in class and independently, spanning image classification (eg. building vision transformers for classifying handwritten digits), semantic segmentation (eg. segmenting aerial views for disaster classification), segmenting face masks from faces for masked facial recognition using SAM), object recognition (eg. correctly placed COVID face mask detection for healthcare), image processing (eg. using PCA to increase clarity in dog pictures, deriving a Gibbs sampling algorithm to restore corrupted images), model performance improvements (eg. using denoising diffusion and knowledge distillation to improve facial landmark detection models), and 3D computer vision (eg. masked 3D facial reconstruction, multi-view 3D scene reconstruction). 
​
Outside of school, I love the outdoors: ocean kayaking, diving, canoeing, rowing, camping, hiking and ziplining. I am PADI-certified for open water diving. ​I have camped in several designated camping sites across California. I also enjoy going on spontaneous road trips and exploring new sights.

I am proficient in several languages and like experimenting with different cuisines to make fusion dishes. 

I am very passionate about the fight against child marriage and human trafficking, and promoting gender equity. In the future, I will continue expanding on my previous work with Girl Scouts and community engagement to promote these causes.
