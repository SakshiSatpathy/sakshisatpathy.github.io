Amnesty International USA   https://www.amnesty.org/en/

(A) Gunn Amnesty International Club, Founder and President: Sep 2015 – April 2019 https://gunnamnesty.strikingly.com

Gunn Amnesty is a chapter of Amnesty International, working to protect human rights in the local and international
community. I planned and led all club activities, including teaching members about human rights issues around the world,
organizing advocacy to increase awareness on domestic and international human rights issues in our high school and local
Bay Area community, and calling out right violations through petitioning and peaceful protest.

Using Amnesty International’s Urgent Action Network, we petitioned foreign ministers, ambassadors and authorities to:
  • uphold rights to free speech, land, religion, race, language and sexuality in various countries including (but not limited to) Iran, China, Turkey, Guatemala and India
  • release those wrongfully imprisoned globally
  • provide adequate medical treatment to all prisoners
  • safeguard individuals at-risk (civilians) facing harassment and/or discrimination in their community
  • Reduce gender-based violence in Nigeria, 
  • human rights abuses (jail without charge, torture) in Sri Lanka, curtailment of solitary confinement and death penalty in California, and advocacy for refugee rights (World Refugee Rights Day: June 20). For refugee rights, helped introduce motions for signs saying “We Welcome Asylum Seekers” in Palo Alto and Cupertino to respective city councils.

We have also engaged in campaigns to:
  • Abolish the death penalty in California
  • Curb solitary confinement, 
  • Celebrate international cultures (eg. Nowruz)
  • Counter bigotry nationally and internationally
  • Ensure police accountability
  • Protect reproductive rights 
  • Protect refugee rights
  • Support peaceful demonstrations: eg. Women’s Marches, Families Belong Together
