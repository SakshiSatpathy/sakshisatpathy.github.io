Girl Scouts

(A) Girl Board Member, Board of Directors, Girl Scouts NorCal Council, April 2017-April 2018 :

I was 1 of 2 Girl Scouts on a 22-adult Board, closely representing the voices of Northern California’s 47,000 Girl Scouts and 31,000 Volunteers. I was an active participant and part of the deciding force behind impactful decisions--eg. evaluating which beloved camp properties to sell, changing GS cookie prices, altering member dues, launching online platforms to better equip members, unrolling new badges, and increasing funding for programs vocationally empowering girls. Additionally, I strategized course of actions to benefit the Girl Scout community, and learned how a non-stock & non-profit organization ran to serve Girl Scouts, from strategic planning to operations to financial management.    https://www.gsnorcal.org 
​
(B) Regional Delegate, Girl Scouts NorCal  Council, Feb 2016-Feb 2019 : 

Collected information, issues and concerns from Girl Scout leaders in Santa Clara region (Region XI) and presented these along with proposed potential solutions to GSNorCal Council Board Members and delegates from other regions at the Annual GS NorCal Board Meeting and other monthly/quarterly GSNorCal  Leadership meetings. 
https://www.gsnorcal.org/en/our-council/about/delegate-governance.html

(C) Fundraised for all expenses (flight tickets, hotel stay, additional expenses)
Total amount fundraised was $2951-- this was for a troop trip to Costa Rica in July 2016. The fundraising was done through dog walking, baby-sitting, Girl Scout cookie and nut sales, yard work, and running errands and buying groceries for the elderly.

(D) High Adventure Girl Scout:
Member of local group called High Adventure Girl Scouts (HAGS), we co-plan and go on weekend trips, enjoying activities like zip lining, cave exploring, dune-bashing, scuba diving, dog-sledding, whitewater rafting, ocean kayaking, I-flying.

(E) Marketing lead for my Palo Alto Girl Scout Troop 60324:
​For all troop-hosted Parent’s Night Outs (fundraising activity for troop), making flyers, reaching out to parents, handling finances and keeping in touch with participants for future Parent’s Night Outs. 

(F) Active member of the Girl Scouts Service and Leadership Team, Palo Alto, (August 2015-May 2019):
Conceptualized, organized and executed the annual “World Thinking Day” event at JLS Middle School, Palo Alto in February (Feb 25 2018, Feb 26 2017, Feb 21 2016) for local Girl Scout troops to learn more and share about their national and international community and passions. We also organize various local community service projects to mobilize Girl Scouts in our community as a force of good in activities ranging from coat drives to environmental stewardship to sorting materials for hospitals, homeless shelters and Art In Action.
